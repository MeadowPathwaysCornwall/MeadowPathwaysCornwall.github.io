Education Otherwise Than At School (EOTAS)

There are some circumstances where children and young people with special educational needs cannot be educated in a formal educational setting. This may be because a placement has broken down, is at risk of breaking down due to the complexity of needs, or because the provision required cannot be delivered within a school setting.

The SEND Code of Practice (para 10.39) states that:

If your child or young person is struggling to access their current setting, and an Education, Health and Care (EHC) plan is in place or being secured, you may wish to consider an EOTAS request to ensure that the special educational provision outlined in the plan can be delivered in a setting other than a school.

What is EOTAS?

Education Otherwise Than At School means the education or special educational provision of children or young people outside of a formal educational setting. It can only be agreed in conjunction with the local authority as part of an EHC needs assessment, a reassessment of needs, or through the EHCP annual review process.

A child or young person accessing EOTAS is not on roll at a school or post-16 institution. Provision may take place at home or in another non-registered setting, depending on the child‚Äôs needs and agreed plan.

Our EOTAS Offer at Meadow Pathways

Meadow Pathways Wellbeing and Education Cornwall provides bespoke Education Otherwise Than At School provision across Cornwall. Our EOTAS offer places wellbeing at the heart of learning and integrates therapeutic support with personalised education. We work closely with families and partner agencies to plan learning that supports emotional safety, builds confidence, and re-engages young people with purposeful learning.

Who We Support

We support children and young people who cannot attend mainstream school for reasons such as social, emotional and mental health needs, special educational needs, school avoidance, or where a tailored approach is needed to re-engage a learner with education. How We Work

Our work begins with listening. We gather information from families, schools and professionals, then use diagnostic assessments and literacy and numeracy profiling to design targeted learning. Sessions blend therapeutic practice with curriculum-linked activities so that learning is meaningful and transferable. We use small group work, one-to-one mentoring, and community-based activities to build skills, confidence and positive connections.
