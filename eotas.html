EOTAS and Community Provision

Meadow Pathways provides tailored Education Otherwise Than At School (EOTAS) and community-based support for children and young people with SEN, SEMH needs, and those affected by educational trauma or bereavement. We offer flexible, relationship-centred programmes designed to rebuild confidence, resilience, and learning readiness.

What we offer

  • Individualised learning plans and small group sessions
  • Therapeutic play, trauma-informed counselling, and wellbeing coaching
  • Practical life skills, outdoor learning, and creative activities
  • School liaison, transition planning and ongoing progress reviews

Referral and Assessment

Referrals can be made by families, schools, or agencies. We use a strengths-based assessment to identify needs, set goals, and co-produce a safe, paced support plan.

Safeguarding and Data

Meadow Pathways follows best-practice safeguarding, confidentiality, and data protection. Relevant safeguarding or medical information should be included in referrals to ensure safety and appropriate support planning.
