Education Otherwise Than At School (EOTAS)

There are some circumstances where children and young people with special educational needs cannot be educated in a formal educational setting. This may be because a placement has broken down, is at risk of breaking down due to the complexity of needs, or because the provision required cannot be delivered within a school setting.

The SEND Code of Practice (para 10.39) states that:

“Local authorities must make arrangements where, for any reason, a child of compulsory school age would not otherwise receive suitable education. Suitable education means efficient education suitable to a child or young person’s age, ability and aptitude, and to any special educational needs they may have. This education must be full time, unless the local authority determines that, for reasons relating to the physical or mental health of the child, a reduced level of education would be in the child’s best interests.”

If your child or young person is struggling to access their current setting, and an Education, Health and Care (EHC) plan is in place or being secured, you may wish to consider an EOTAS request to ensure that the special educational provision outlined in the plan can be delivered in a setting other than a school.

What EOTAS can include

  • Online or virtual learning
  • Home tuition
  • Tuition centres or specialist learning hubs
  • Hospital schooling
  • Therapeutic input such as speech and language therapy, occupational therapy, physiotherapy, counselling, or play therapy

Our EOTAS offer at Meadow Pathways

Meadow Pathways provides bespoke Education Otherwise Than At School provision across Cornwall. Our EOTAS offer places wellbeing at the heart of learning and integrates therapeutic support with personalised education. We work closely with families and partner agencies to plan learning that supports emotional safety, builds confidence, and re-engages young people with purposeful learning.

How we work

Our work begins with listening. We gather information from families, schools and professionals, then use diagnostic assessments and literacy and numeracy profiling to design targeted learning. Sessions blend therapeutic practice with curriculum-linked activities so that learning is meaningful and transferable. We use small group work, one-to-one mentoring, and community-based activities to build skills, confidence and positive connections.

What we offer

  • Mentoring and Advocacy
  • Tailored Learning Packages
  • Life Skills and Independence Training
  • Diagnostic Assessments
  • SEMH Interventions
  • Wellbeing and PSHE Packages
  • Transitional Services
  • Therapeutic and Curriculum-Linked Activities

Activities are chosen with each learner to reflect their interests and provide opportunities for success, wellbeing and skills development. Activities are linked to the curriculum and supported by ASDAN qualifications and AQA Unit Awards.
