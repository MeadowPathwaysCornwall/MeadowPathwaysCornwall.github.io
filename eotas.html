What is EOTAS?

Education Otherwise Than At School (EOTAS) describes tailored education for learners who cannot access mainstream school provision for reasons including significant anxiety, medical needs, or educational trauma. Meadow Pathways offers small‑group and one‑to‑one programmes linked to EHCP outcomes and accreditation pathways.

How we work

  • Individual learning plans co‑produced with families, schools and local authorities
  • Therapeutic sessions integrated with curriculum objectives
  • Outdoor and community settings used to reduce stress and build practical skills
  • Flexible timetables that respect pacing and emotional safety

Accreditation and assessment

We support recognised accreditation where appropriate (AQA Unit Awards, ASDAN) and provide clear progress reporting aligned to EHCP outcomes and individual goals.

Referral considerations

When making a referral, please include recent reports, risk information, and any relevant EHCP evidence. Use our referral form or contact the team to discuss complex cases before submission.
