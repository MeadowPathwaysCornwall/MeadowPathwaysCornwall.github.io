What is EOTAS?

EOTAS stands for Education Otherwise Than At School. It is a specialised educational provision arranged by the Local Authority for children and young people with an Education, Health and Care Plan (EHCP) whose needs cannot be met within a traditional school environment.

At Meadow Pathways Well-being and Education, we offer EOTAS packages that are thoughtful, creative, and tailored to the individual. Our provision supports children across Cornwall, creating personalised paths to learning, well-being, and growth.

Legal Basis

EOTAS is made possible by Section 61 of the Children and Families Act 2014. Cornwall Council can approve education outside of school when:

“... it would be inappropriate for the provision to be made in a school or post-16 institution.”

How is it different from Elective Home Education?

Elective Home Education is a parent-led choice and removes the Local Authority's duty to provide the EHCP provision. In contrast, EOTAS is a Local Authority decision that education should take place outside school — but it still requires the LA to ensure all EHCP provision is delivered.

Accessing EOTAS in Cornwall

  • Your child must have an EHCP.
  • Professionals and families must provide evidence that no school can meet their needs.
  • The Cornwall SEND team must agree that EOTAS is appropriate.
  • An EOTAS Coordinator will help build the educational package.

For more information, visit the Cornwall Local Offer website or contact your SEND caseworker.

What might an EOTAS package include?

EOTAS packages can include up to 25 hours per week during term time. Examples include:

  • One-to-one tuition
  • Online or home-based learning
  • Life skills development
  • Mentoring and therapeutic support
  • Creative or vocational education
