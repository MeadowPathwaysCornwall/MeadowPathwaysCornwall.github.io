What is EOTAS?

EOTAS stands for Education Otherwise Than At School. It is a tailored education provision arranged by the Local Authority for children and young people who have an Education, Health and Care Plan (EHCP) but cannot have their needs met in a mainstream or special school setting.

At Meadow Pathways Well-being and Education, we specialise in delivering EOTAS packages across Cornwall that are creative, compassionate, and entirely bespoke — reflecting the unique journey of each young person we support.

EOTAS is not a parent-led choice like Elective Home Education — it is a legal arrangement, agreed by Cornwall Council, when professionals agree that school is not appropriate and alternative provision is required.

How does EOTAS work?

Under Section 61 of the Children and Families Act 2014, Cornwall Council can agree to provide education outside of a school when it is satisfied that:

“... it would be inappropriate for the provision to be made in a school or post-16 institution.”

This decision follows input from relevant professionals, the young person’s views, and the voice of the family.

EOTAS is always considered a last resort, used only when all other school-based options and support have been explored and found unsuitable.

How to Access EOTAS in Cornwall

To access EOTAS in Cornwall:

  • Your child must have an EHCP.
  • Professionals and family must provide clear evidence that no suitable educational institution can meet their needs.
  • Cornwall Council’s SEND team will assess whether EOTAS is appropriate.
  • If agreed, a dedicated EOTAS coordinator will help develop a bespoke education package that meets the outcomes in the child’s EHCP.
  • This may include completing an EOTAS Budget Proposal form to outline the plan and costings.

You can find more information on the Cornwall Local Offer website or contact your child’s SEND caseworker directly.

What might an EOTAS package include?

A full-time EOTAS package can be up to 25 hours per week during term time and may include:

    • 1:1 tuition
    • Online learning
    • Life skills and well-being sessions
    • Mentoring and emotional support
    • Therapeutic or SEMH interventions
    • Vocational training or off-site learning

At Meadow Pathways, we work closely with families and the Local Authority to design provision that is meaningful, engaging, and rooted in well-being.
