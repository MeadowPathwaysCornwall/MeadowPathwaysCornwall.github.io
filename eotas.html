There are circumstances where children and young people with SEND cannot be educated in a mainstream school. This may be due to placement breakdown, complexity of needs, or provision required that cannot be delivered in school.

Local authorities must ensure suitable education is provided for any child who would not otherwise receive it. Suitable education is efficient and tailored to age, ability, aptitude, and special educational needs. It should be full time unless health reasons mean a reduced level is in the childâ€™s best interests.

If a child cannot attend a mainstream setting and an EHCP is in place or being secured, you may consider an EOTAS request so provision can be delivered outside school.
