There are some circumstances where children and young people with special educational needs cannot be educated in a formal educational setting. This may be because a placement has broken down, is at risk of breaking down due to the complexity of needs, or because the provision required cannot be delivered within a school setting.

The SEND Code of Practice (para 10.39) states:

Our EOTAS approach

We provide bespoke EOTAS packages for children and young people with SEN, SEMH, educational trauma, bereavement, and Emotionally Based School Non-Attendance (EBSNA). Support is relational, flexible, and built around trust, safety, and gradual re-engagement in learning aligned to individual needs.

  • 1:1 therapeutic support and play therapy
  • Nature-based, creative, and practical learning
  • Clear objectives and regular review
  • Coordination with families, schools, and services

Activities and learning

Our EOTAS provision blends therapeutic practice with curriculum-linked activities. Examples include:

  • Woodwork skills
  • Gardening and horticulture
  • Crafting pottery and art
  • Cooking and nutrition
  • Exercise and fitness
  • Animal care
  • Community volunteering
  • Forest & beach skills
  • Rock climbing & outdoor pursuits
