EOTAS guidance (overview)

EOTAS describes arrangements where a local authority agrees that education will be provided somewhere other than a school or post‚Äë16 institution, when it would be inappropriate for provision to be made there. Decisions are made on an individual basis and should be supported by evidence that a school or college cannot meet the child or young person‚Äôs needs.

Schools and colleges remain the default route for most learners, and they can deliver flexible programmes across on‚Äësite and off‚Äësite settings. Where these avenues have been explored and are not appropriate, EOTAS may be considered as part of an EHCP process and reviewed regularly to ensure needs are met and outcomes are pursued.

Funding and scope

An EOTAS package should deliver the educational requirements identified in the EHCP. If elements cannot be delivered, the plan may need to be reviewed. Funding should be used for education and the support required to access it; it is monitored to ensure effective and fair use of public funds.

Examples of items typically not covered include household bills, clothing, non‚Äëeducational therapies, or costs not required to access provision. Transport and meals may be considered where eligibility applies and where necessary for attendance.
