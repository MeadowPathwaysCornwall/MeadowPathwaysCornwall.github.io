Education Otherwise Than At School (EOTAS)

Flexible, creative provision tailored to each child’s journey

What is EOTAS?

EOTAS stands for Education Otherwise Than At School. It is a provision arranged by the Local Authority for children and young people who cannot attend school, even with additional support. This may be due to medical needs, trauma, Emotionally Based School Non-Attendance (EBSNA), or other complex circumstances.

EOTAS is not a “one size fits all” solution. It is a flexible, personalised approach that ensures every child continues to access education in a way that feels safe, meaningful, and achievable.

Who is EOTAS for?

  • Children unable to attend school due to medical, emotional, or mental health needs
  • Young people experiencing trauma or bereavement affecting engagement
  • Those awaiting specialist placements or assessments
  • Children needing a highly individualised approach to learning and wellbeing

How Meadow Pathways Supports Families

We provide trauma-informed, relational, and creative EOTAS provision, guided by the SPACE ethos — Safety, Presence, Attunement, Connection, and Empowerment. We meet children where they are, through play, nature-based learning, therapeutic support, or curriculum re-engagement.

We collaborate closely with families, schools, and the Local Authority to ensure support is joined-up and responsive. Every child’s journey is unique, and we walk alongside them with care, flexibility, and hope.

The Referral Process

Referrals for EOTAS are usually made through the Local Authority, often following an EHCP review or when school-based support is no longer sufficient. Families and professionals can also contact us directly to discuss options and explore whether EOTAS may be appropriate.

To make a referral, please visit our Referral Form page or get in touch via our Contact Us page.
