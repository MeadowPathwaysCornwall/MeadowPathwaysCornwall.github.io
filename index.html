Our Aims

Our aims are to foster resilience, independence, well-being, honesty, integrity, friendship, and kindness in every individual we support.

Welcome to Meadow Pathways Cornwall

Meadow Pathways Cornwall is dedicated to supporting children and young people through a holistic, trauma-informed approach to education and well-being. Based in the heart of Cornwall, we specialise in providing nurturing, individualised support for those who may not thrive in mainstream school environments, including learners with neurodivergent profiles, trauma experiences, EBSNA (Emotionally Based School Non-Attendance), bereavement, and those with Education Health and Care Plans (EHCPs).

Our team is passionate about creating a safe, inclusive, and supportive environment where every child and young person is empowered to flourish. We work closely with families, local authorities, schools, and a wide range of specialist services to ensure a truly collaborative approach to well-being and education.

We offer a range of bespoke packages, including Education Otherwise Than At School (EOTAS) provisions, on-site therapeutic education, and outreach into the community. Our flexible, creative programmes are designed to ignite curiosity, build confidence, and enable every learner to develop their strengths and interests.

Our Values

  • Child-Centered: Every learner’s journey is unique. We place children and young people at the heart of everything we do.
  • Trauma-Informed: Our staff are trained in trauma-sensitive approaches, ensuring safety and trust for all.
  • Collaboration: We work in partnership with families, professionals, and the wider community.
  • Inclusion: We celebrate diversity and champion the rights of all learners to be heard and respected.
  • Well-being: Emotional, social, and mental well-being are central to our practice.

What We Offer

  • Bespoke EOTAS packages for children and young people unable to access mainstream education
  • On-site therapeutic education and well-being sessions
  • Outreach and community-based learning
  • Support for families and carers
  • Multi-agency working with local authorities, schools, and specialist services
