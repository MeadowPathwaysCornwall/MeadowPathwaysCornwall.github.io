Staff guidance and secure access

How to request access, securely handle credentials and work with protected resources

Request staff access Protected resources

Overview

Meadow Pathways treats staff credentials and protected documents with high priority. We do not publish passwords or private links on public pages. Staff access is granted after a formal request and confirmation from the director.

How to request access

  1. Complete the staff access request form on the Staff page. Include your full name, work email and role.
  2. Requests are reviewed and approved by the director. You will receive confirmation by email when access is granted.
  3. Approved staff will be given secure instructions for accessing protected resources; we use secure links and credential tools rather than publishing passwords.

Best practice for credentials

  • Never store passwords in plain text, in repo files or on shared drives without appropriate encryption.
  • Use a password manager approved by the organisation to store and share credentials securely.
  • Choose strong, unique passwords and enable multi-factor authentication (MFA) wherever available.
  • If you suspect credentials have been compromised, notify the director immediately and change access via the provider's secure flow.

Accessing protected documents

Protected documents (staff records, safeguarding logs, supervision notes) are stored in a secured area. Access pathways and expiry for shared links are managed centrally.

Support and queries

For questions about secure access, password managers or IT arrangements, contact the director or operations lead. They will advise on approved tools and any setup help required.

Note: Never include private account details in email. We will never ask staff to share passwords via email.
