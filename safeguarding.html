Safeguarding and Child Protection

Meadow Pathways is committed to safeguarding and protecting the welfare of children and young people. We work to the highest standards of practice and expect all staff and partners to follow our safeguarding procedures.

Our approach

We ensure all staff are DBS-checked, trained in safeguarding and know how to report concerns. We maintain clear records, follow data protection guidance when sharing information, and work closely with local safeguarding partners to keep children safe.

Reporting concerns

If you have an immediate safeguarding concern contact your local authority safeguarding team or the police. For all concerns relating to children supported by Meadow Pathways, report to Michelle or Zoe as our designated leads and follow internal reporting procedures.
