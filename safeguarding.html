Our Commitment to Safeguarding

At Meadow Pathways, safeguarding is central. We create emotionally safe, nurturing environments and follow statutory guidance including KCSIE.

What is Safeguarding and Child Protection?

Safeguarding is the umbrella term for everything we do to keep children and young people safe from harm, both inside and outside of education. It means creating environments where children feel secure, valued, and listened to, and where their wellbeing is always the highest priority. Child protection is a vital part of safeguarding — it refers specifically to the actions taken to protect children who are at risk of significant harm, abuse, or neglect.

Why Meadow Pathways Adheres to Safeguarding Principles

At Meadow Pathways, safeguarding is not just a statutory duty, it is a core part of who we are. Many of the children and families we support have experienced trauma, anxiety, or barriers to education. By embedding safeguarding into every aspect of our practice, we ensure that:

  • Children are protected from harm and given the chance to thrive in safe, nurturing spaces.
  • Families can trust that their children’s wellbeing is at the heart of every decision we make.
  • Staff are guided by clear policies, professional training, and a culture of vigilance and care.
  • We meet and exceed national guidance, including Keeping Children Safe in Education (KCSIE), so that our provision is consistent, transparent, and accountable.

Safeguarding is everyone’s responsibility. By working together with families, schools, and the wider community, Meadow Pathways ensures that every child is seen, heard, and supported to grow with confidence and resilience.
