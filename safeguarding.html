Safeguarding

Meadow Pathways is committed to safeguarding and promoting the welfare of children and young people. We believe that every child has the right to feel safe, valued, and supported. Our safeguarding practices are embedded in everything we do, from therapeutic education to community engagement.

Our Safeguarding Principles

  • Child-centred approach – We listen to children and take their views seriously.
  • Early intervention – We identify concerns early and respond promptly.
  • Multi-agency collaboration – We work closely with families, schools, and professionals.
  • Safe environments – We create emotionally and physically safe spaces for learning and growth.
  • Clear procedures – Our safeguarding policies are robust, transparent, and regularly reviewed.

Designated Safeguarding Leads

Michelle Pascoe and Zoe Waitz are our Designated Safeguarding Leads. They are responsible for overseeing safeguarding practices and ensuring that concerns are addressed appropriately and sensitively.

Reporting a Concern

If you have a safeguarding concern, please contact Michelle or Zoe directly:

  • Email: Michelle.Pascoe@meadowpathwayscornwall.com
  • Email: Zoe.Waitz@meadowpathwayscornwall.com

We take all concerns seriously and act in accordance with statutory guidance and best practice.
