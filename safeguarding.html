Safeguarding and Child Protection

Safeguarding means protecting children and young people from harm, promoting their welfare, and ensuring they grow up in safe, supportive environments. It includes preventing abuse, recognising signs of concern early, responding promptly and appropriately, and working together with families and statutory agencies to keep children safe.

Why Safeguarding Matters

Every child has the right to feel safe and to be protected from neglect, abuse and exploitation. Safeguarding helps children to thrive, supports families, reduces long-term harm, and builds trusting relationships between professionals and communities.
