Safeguarding and Child Protection

Protecting children and young people with clarity, care, and professional responsibility.

Safeguarding is central to everything we do at Meadow Pathways. It means protecting children and young people from harm, promoting their welfare, and ensuring they grow up in safe, supportive environments.

Safeguarding includes preventing abuse, recognising early signs of concern, responding promptly and appropriately, and working together with families and statutory agencies to keep children safe.

Why safeguarding matters

Every child has the right to feel safe and to be protected from neglect, abuse, and exploitation. Effective safeguarding ensures children can thrive, supports families, reduces long-term harm, and builds trusting relationships between professionals and the communities we work with.

Need Help Right Now?

If you think a child is in immediate danger, contact the police by dialling 999.

For urgent safeguarding concerns in Cornwall, contact the Multi Agency Referral Unit (MARU) on 0300 123 1116 or email multiagencyreferralunit@cornwall.gov.uk.
