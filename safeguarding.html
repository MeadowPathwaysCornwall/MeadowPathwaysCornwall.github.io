Safeguarding and Child Protection

Safeguarding is central to Meadow Pathways. It means protecting children and young people from harm, promoting their welfare, and ensuring they grow up in safe, supportive environments. Safeguarding includes preventing abuse, recognising signs of concern early, responding promptly and appropriately, and working together with families and statutory agencies to keep children safe.

Our staff are trained to be alert to risk, to listen carefully to children and families, to act on concerns, and to follow clear reporting procedures. We balance compassion with professional responsibility, ensuring that every concern is taken seriously and acted upon in line with statutory guidance and local safeguarding arrangements.

Why safeguarding matters

Every child has the right to feel safe and to be protected from neglect, abuse and exploitation. Safeguarding helps children to thrive, supports families, reduces long-term harm, and builds trusting relationships between professionals and communities. Robust safeguarding practice also protects staff and the organisation by ensuring concerns are handled transparently and professionally.
