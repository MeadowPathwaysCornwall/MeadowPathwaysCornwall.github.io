Safeguarding

Safeguarding

At Meadow Pathways Well-being and Education, safeguarding is at the heart of everything we do. We are fully committed to creating and maintaining a safe, supportive, and nurturing environment for all children, young people, staff, and visitors—both on-site and in the wider community.

We work with children of all ages, including those with neurodiverse needs, trauma-related experiences, EBSNA (Emotionally Based School Non-Attendance), bereavement, and those with Educational Health and Care Plans (EHCPs). We understand that every child’s journey is unique, and safeguarding must be responsive to their individual circumstances. We are proud of our strong Cornish ties and work closely with the Local Authority and a wide range of specialist support services to ensure that no child is left unsupported.

Our safeguarding practices are rooted in the most current statutory guidance, including Keeping Children Safe in Education and Working Together to Safeguard Children. These are reflected in all our policies, which are reviewed regularly and updated to meet evolving standards.

Safeguarding is everyone’s responsibility at Meadow Pathways. All staff, volunteers, and visitors are expected to uphold our safeguarding principles. We promote a strong culture of vigilance and accountability, ensuring that all staff receive high-quality, ongoing training in safeguarding and child protection.

Designated Safeguarding Lead: Michelle Pascoe has nearly a decade of experience as a DSL and has undertaken extensive safeguarding training, including through Cornwall Local Authority. She is supported by a Deputy DSL and a wider team, all committed to early intervention and multi-agency working.

Where staff use personal mobile phones to support their role, appropriate filters, data protection, and monitoring procedures are in place to maintain professional boundaries and comply with our safeguarding expectations.

  • Our Commitment:
    • Ensuring the safety and wellbeing of every learner is our highest priority.
    • We embed safeguarding into every aspect of our practice, from curriculum to community-based learning.
    • We actively promote a culture of openness, trust, and respect among staff and learners alike.
  • Reporting Concerns:
    • If you are concerned about a child or young person, please contact our Designated Safeguarding Lead – Michelle Pascoe – without delay.
    • Contact details can be found on our Contact Us page.
  • Policies and Training:
    • All staff receive regular, comprehensive safeguarding and child protection training.
    • Our policies are regularly reviewed in line with up-to-date legislation and local authority guidance.
    • We work in full compliance with national safeguarding expectations and best practice frameworks.
