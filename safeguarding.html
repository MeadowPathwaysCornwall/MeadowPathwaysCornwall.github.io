Safeguarding

Safety, trust, and early intervention at the heart of our work

Safeguarding is central to everything we do at Meadow Pathways. We are committed to creating safe, nurturing environments where children and young people feel protected, respected, and heard.

All staff hold enhanced DBS checks and complete regular safeguarding training. We follow clear policies and procedures to identify concerns early, respond appropriately, and work collaboratively with families and professionals.

Our safeguarding approach is trauma-informed, relational, and proactive. We prioritise emotional safety, consistency, and trust-building in every interaction.

Key principles

  • Early identification and intervention
  • Clear communication and documentation
  • Multi-agency collaboration
  • Respect for the child’s voice and lived experience
  • Ongoing staff training and supervision
