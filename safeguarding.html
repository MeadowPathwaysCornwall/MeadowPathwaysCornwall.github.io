Safeguarding at Meadow Pathways

Meadow Pathways is committed to protecting the welfare of children and young people. We follow statutory guidance and local partnership protocols to ensure timely, proportionate and safe responses to any concerns.

Reporting concerns

  1. Ensure immediate safety — if urgent, contact emergency services first.
  2. Record the concern using Meadow Pathways record keeping guidance.
  3. Contact the DSL or Deputy DSL without delay.
  4. Where appropriate, refer to statutory services and the local safeguarding partnership.
If this is an urgent safeguarding concern, contact statutory services immediately and then notify the Meadow Pathways DSL.

Local partnership and guidance

We work with the Cornwall, Isles of Scilly safeguarding partnership and follow Keeping Children Safe in Education and local multi-agency procedures. Key external resources are available to staff via the protected resources area.

Safeguarding training and records

All staff complete safeguarding training appropriate to their role. Training records and recent updates are stored in the protected resources for authorised staff only.
