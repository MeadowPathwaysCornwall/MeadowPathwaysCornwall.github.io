Safeguarding and Child Protection

Meadow Pathways places the safety, wellbeing and welfare of children and young people at the centre of everything we do. We are committed to preventing harm, recognising needs early and taking proportionate action to keep learners safe. Safeguarding is the shared responsibility of every adult who works with children, families and partner agencies.

Our safeguarding principles

  • Child first — the child’s welfare is paramount and we always consider what is in the best interests of the child.
  • Prevention — we create safe, nurturing environments and use trauma-informed practice to reduce risk.
  • Early help — we respond quickly to emerging needs and work with families and partners to provide timely support.
  • Proportionality — responses are proportionate to the level of need or risk identified.
  • Confidentiality — we share information on a need-to-know basis to protect children while respecting family privacy.
  • Multi-agency working — we collaborate with local safeguarding partners, health and education services to achieve the best outcomes for children.

Reporting concerns

If you are concerned that a child or young person is being harmed, abused or neglected, act without delay. In an emergency where a child is at immediate risk, contact the emergency services. For non-emergencies, report concerns to the Designated Safeguarding Lead at Meadow Pathways and, where required, to the local authority children's social care team and other relevant partners.

How we keep children safe

  • All staff receive regular safeguarding training and updates, including KCSIE essentials.
  • We operate safer recruitment checks for all staff and volunteers where appropriate.
  • We maintain clear safeguarding records and follow local thresholds for referral and multi-agency planning.
  • We apply appropriate staff:child ratios and risk-assess all activities, trips and community sessions.
  • We promote online safety and support families with guidance about managing digital risks.
  • We ensure staff understand professional boundaries and have access to supervision and wellbeing support.

Safeguarding in our practice

Our therapeutic and educational work is trauma-informed and relationship-based. We use emotion coaching, attachment-aware approaches and therapeutic interventions to help children build regulation skills and resilience. When safeguarding concerns intersect with therapeutic need we coordinate with statutory agencies to ensure children receive the right help at the right time.

Confidentiality and information sharing

We handle safeguarding information sensitively and only share what is necessary to protect a child. We follow local and national guidance on information sharing and retain records securely in line with our retention policy.

Photographs and media

We obtain appropriate permissions from parents or carers before using photographs or media that identify children. Where images are used for publicity or learning records, we follow our photography and media policy and keep storage secure.

Safer recruitment and staff conduct

Meadow Pathways follows safer recruitment principles including identity verification, reference checks and appropriate pre-employment checks. Staff receive induction and ongoing training to ensure they understand their safeguarding responsibilities.

If you have a concern about a child

  1. Do not delay — if a child is at immediate risk contact the emergency services.
  2. Record the concern using clear, factual language and report it to the DSL.
  3. If the concern requires, make a referral to the local authority children's social care following local thresholds.
  4. Please keep the DSL informed of any actions and maintain appropriate confidentiality.
