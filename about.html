About Us

Meadow Pathways Cornwall is a specialist provider dedicated to supporting children and young people who may not thrive in mainstream education environments. We use a trauma-informed, holistic approach to nurture the emotional, social, and educational needs of each learner.

Meet the Team

At Meadow Pathways Cornwall Education, we are passionate about providing nurturing, personalised education and support for children with SEMH needs. Our leadership team brings together a wealth of experience, specialist training, and a shared commitment to helping every child thrive.

Michelle Pascoe – Co-Founder / Director of Therapies

Michelle is a qualified Person-Centred Counsellor and Postgraduate Play Therapist. taking a lead on safeguarding, compliance and staff supervision. She supports the wellbeing of all students and works closely with the director of education to integrate therapeutic and educational approaches. With over 20 years of experience working with children and young people dealing with social, emotional, and mental health challenges, Michelle has held senior roles in safeguarding and pastoral care. Renowned for their warm, trauma-informed approach, Michelle leads the therapeutic support at Meadow Pathways Cornwall Education, ensuring our work is always rooted in compassion, inclusion, and safety.

Zoe Waitz – Co-Founder / Director of Education

Zoe is a qualified teacher with a background in special education and alternative provision Zoe brings years of experience in education and Forest School/outdoor education interventions. As the Director of Education for Meadow Pathways, Zoe is responsible for educational standards, planning, and directly working with the children. She ensures the delivery of high-quality, personalised education that is shaped by each learner's strengths, challenges, and interests.She has worked closely with learners who struggle in mainstream settings, including those with anxiety, school avoidance, and complex behavioural needs. Zoe brings calm consistency, creative planning, and deep understanding to her role — helping children rebuild confidence, re-engage with learning, and achieve positive outcomes.

Our team is committed to creating safe, inclusive spaces that celebrate neurodiversity and individual strengths. We provide personalised support to children and young people facing challenges such as trauma, emotional-based school non-attendance (EBSNA), bereavement, and complex educational health and care needs.
