About Meadow Pathways

Meadow Pathways provides trauma informed, person‑centred education and wellbeing support for children and young people across Cornwall. We specialise in supporting those with SEND, SEMH, Emotionally Based School Non Attendance (EBSNA), bereavement, and the effects of educational trauma. Our approach places wellbeing at the heart of learning and recovery.

Our Ethos

We create individualised programmes that meet learners where they are — emotionally, socially and academically — combining therapeutic practice with creative, curriculum‑linked activities. Safety, trust, creativity and belonging guide every decision we make.

Meet the team

Michelle Pascoe

Founding director and lead therapist. Michelle is a qualified counsellor and play therapist with over 20 years’ experience, specialising in trauma‑informed and person‑centred practice. She leads our therapeutic work and partnerships.

Zoe Waitz

Co‑director and education lead. Zoe supports curriculum design and delivery, ensuring bespoke learning pathways are achievable, accredited and trauma informed.

Our approach

We blend therapeutic interventions with practical, community‑based learning. Sessions are tailored to the learner's interests, linked to accreditation where appropriate, and delivered by skilled staff trained in trauma informed practice.
