About Meadow Pathways

Guiding Individual Journeys with Care and Purpose

Our commitment

We place safety, trust and creativity at the centre of our work with children, families and partners.

Who we are

Meadow Pathways provides trauma informed, person-centred education and wellbeing support for children and young people across Cornwall. We specialise in supporting those with SEND, SEMH, Emotionally Based School Non Attendance (EBSNA), bereavement, and the effects of educational trauma. Our ethos is rooted in safety, trust, creativity, and belonging.

We believe that every child deserves the opportunity to thrive, no matter their background or previous experiences. Our holistic approach blends therapeutic practice with flexible, creative education that meets learners where they are — emotionally, socially, and academically.

Working in partnership with families, schools, and local authorities, we design individualised programmes that nurture confidence, rebuild relationships with learning, and promote emotional resilience. Whether through outdoor exploration, creative arts, or one-to-one mentoring, our work empowers children and young people to rediscover their strengths and find joy in learning.

At Meadow Pathways, we recognise that healing and education go hand in hand. By fostering an environment where every voice is valued and every journey respected, we help our learners grow not only in knowledge but in wellbeing, independence, and self-belief.

What we offer

  • 🧑‍🤝‍🧑 Mentoring and Advocacy — Consistent relational support.

Meet the team

Michelle Pascoe

Michelle is a founding director and Lead Therapist for Meadow Pathways Wellbeing and Education Cornwall. She supports children and young people across the community who experience Special Educational Needs (SEN), Social, Emotional and Mental Health (SEMH) needs, educational trauma, bereavement, and Emotionally Based School Non‑Attendance (EBSNA).

With over 20 years of experience, Michelle is a qualified counsellor and play therapist. She spent more than a decade in an independent SEN school, beginning as a HLTA and progressing to senior leadership, where she served as Designated Safeguarding Lead for over seven years.

Zoe Waitz

Zoe Waitz is a founding director of Meadow Pathways Wellbeing and Education Cornwall. Her leadership is rooted in genuine care and a belief that nature and relationship-based work supports lasting change.
