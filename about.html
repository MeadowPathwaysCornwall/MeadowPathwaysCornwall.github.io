About Meadow Pathways

We provide trauma-informed, person-centred support for children and young people across Cornwall. We specialise in working with those experiencing SEMH needs, SEND, Emotionally Based School Non-Attendance, bereavement, and the effects of educational trauma.

What we offer

  • Mentoring and Advocacy — Consistent relational support.
  • Tailored Learning Packages — Flexible academic pathways.
  • Life Skills and Independence — Functional skills and community engagement.
  • Diagnostic Assessments — Literacy and numeracy profiling.
  • SEMH Interventions — Trauma-informed strategies.
  • Wellbeing and PSHE — Creative therapeutic sessions.
  • Transitional Services — Reintegration and onward pathways.
  • Therapeutic and Curriculum-Linked Activities — Nature-based bush craft, walking, fitness, water-based activities, fishing, climbing, cooking, play, biking, and more. Activities are linked to the curriculum and supported by ASDAN and AQA Unit Awards.

Our approach

We build trusting relationships through warmth, empathy, and consistency. Guided by SPACE — Safety, Playfulness, Acceptance, Curiosity, and Empathy — we use play therapy, creative activities, and community-based learning to help children re-engage, feel safe, and grow in confidence.
