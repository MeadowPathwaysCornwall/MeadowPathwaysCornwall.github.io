About Michelle

Michelle is a qualified person-centred counsellor and trained play therapist, with extensive experience supporting children with Special Educational Needs and Social, Emotional, and Mental Health (SEMH) needs. She ensures that every child’s voice is heard and valued.

With around 20 years of experience working with neurodiverse children and those facing emotional and behavioural challenges, Michelle has developed a deep understanding of each child’s individual journey. She recognises the importance of children finding their own path and is committed to supporting them with empathy, care, and ongoing professional development in therapeutic practice.

Zoe Waitz

Zoe brings years of experience in education and Forest School/outdoor education interventions. She is passionate about helping young people reach their potential and is committed to safeguarding and inclusive practice.

With over a decade of experience working with neurodiverse children and those with Social, Emotional and Mental Health (SEMH) needs, Zoe has developed a strong understanding of how to support children who may find traditional school environments challenging, particularly due to social or emotional barriers. She has a long-standing commitment to helping learners re-engage with education in a way that suits their individual needs.

As the Director of Education for Meadow Pathways, Zoe is responsible for educational standards, planning, and directly working with the children. She ensures the delivery of high-quality, personalised education that is shaped by each learner's strengths, challenges, and interests. Zoe works closely with families, professionals, and the wider team to build trusting relationships and create flexible pathways that enable young people to thrive.
