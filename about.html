About Meadow Pathways

We’re a wellbeing‑centred education community in Cornwall, built on care, connection, and inclusive, therapeutic practice.

Who we are

Meadow Pathways Wellbeing and Education Cornwall is an organisation built on the belief that every child deserves to feel safe, seen, and supported. We offer inclusive, therapeutic education for children and young people with SEND and SEMH needs, placing wellbeing at the centre of everything we do.

M

Michelle — Founding director and lead therapist

EOTAS and Meadow Pathways Wellbeing and Education Cornwall

Michelle is a founding director and the lead therapist at Meadow Pathways. A qualified counsellor and play therapist with over twenty years’ experience, she supports children and young people across the community, including those with SEN, SEMH, educational trauma, bereavement, and Emotionally Based School Non‑Attendance (EBSNA). Her work is trauma‑informed and person‑centred, guided by the SPACE ethos and a deep commitment to emotional safety and trust.

Michelle’s approach is flexible and creative, meeting each child at their own pace—whether in the gym, at the beach, or in a quiet learning space to rebuild confidence and school‑readiness. She partners closely with families, shaping support that’s warm, authentic, and genuinely responsive to each young person’s journey.

Areas of expertise

Trauma‑informed counselling Play therapy SPACE, EBSNA, TIS Emotion coaching Community‑based support

Ethos and communication

Michelle believes that every detail—from first contact to visual branding—should feel welcoming and emotionally safe. She is highly collaborative and detail‑oriented, ensuring communications reflect the organisation’s values with clarity, warmth, and integrity.

Beyond work, Michelle finds energy in nature, travel, family time, and walking her dogs, Pixie and Ivey—reminders of the simple joys that sustain resilience.

Z

Zoe Waitz — Founding director

Meadow Pathways Wellbeing and Education Cornwall

Zoe Waitz is a founding director of Meadow Pathways, an organisation built on the belief that every child deserves to feel safe, seen, and supported. Meadow Pathways offers inclusive, therapeutic education for children and young people with SEND and SEMH needs, placing wellbeing at the centre of everything it does.

Leadership and ethos

Zoe’s leadership is rooted in genuine care. She understands that meaningful change happens not just through policies and plans, but through everyday moments of connection with learners, families, and the dedicated staff who walk alongside them. Her approach weaves compassion into strategic decisions, ensuring that every layer of the organisation reflects the values it stands for.

Career journey

Zoe began her career as a teaching assistant, driven by a passion for outdoor learning and physical education. She qualified as a PE teacher and quickly recognised the value of working closely with families and other specialists to support each young person. This led to pastoral and senior leadership in specialist education, deepening her commitment to inclusive, wellbeing‑focused practice.

She brings deep insight into the emotional and practical realities faced by vulnerable learners and the teams who support them. That lived understanding now shapes every aspect of Meadow Pathways—from curriculum to compliance, to the warmth of a morning welcome.

Connection with nature

Nature is a constant thread in Zoe’s work. As a former outdoor education instructor, she sees the outdoors not just as a backdrop, but as a space that offers something different to everyone. Whether it’s a walk along the Cornish coast, a forest school session, or cooking with foraged ingredients, Zoe believes that nature offers regulation, joy, and connection for young people, staff, and the wider community. It’s this blend of care, creativity, and purpose that makes Meadow Pathways feel not just like a service, but a place to belong.

Our values

  • Safety: Emotional and physical safety comes first, always.
  • Connection: Relationships are the foundation of meaningful learning and change.
  • Inclusion: We meet each child where they are, with dignity and respect.
  • Nature: The outdoors offers regulation, joy, and belonging.
  • Practice: Compassion woven through decisions—curriculum to compliance.
