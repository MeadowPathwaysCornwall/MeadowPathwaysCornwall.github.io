About Meadow Pathways

Guiding Individual Journeys with Care and Purpose

Who we are

Meadow Pathways provides trauma‑informed, person‑centred education and wellbeing support for children and young people across Cornwall. We specialise in SEND, SEMH, Emotionally Based School Non‑Attendance (EBSNA), bereavement, and the effects of educational trauma. Our ethos is rooted in safety, trust, creativity, and belonging.

We believe every child deserves the opportunity to thrive. Our holistic approach blends therapeutic practice with flexible, creative education that meets learners where they are — emotionally, socially, and academically.

Working in partnership with families, schools, and local authorities, we design individualised programmes that nurture confidence, rebuild relationships with learning, and promote emotional resilience.

Healing and education go hand in hand. We foster environments where every voice is valued and every journey respected, so learners grow in knowledge, wellbeing, independence, and self‑belief.

What we offer

  • Mentoring and Advocacy — Consistent relational support.
  • Tailored Learning Packages — Flexible academic pathways.
  • Life Skills and Independence — Functional skills and community engagement.
  • Diagnostic Assessments — Literacy and numeracy profiling.
  • SEMH Interventions — Trauma‑informed interventions.
  • Wellbeing and PSHE — Creative therapeutic sessions.
  • Transitional Services — Reintegration and onward pathways.
  • Therapeutic & Curriculum Linked Activities — Nature, fitness, water‑based activities, fishing, climbing, cooking, play, biking, and more; linked to ASDAN and AQA Unit Awards.
