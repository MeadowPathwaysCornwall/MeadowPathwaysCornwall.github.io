About Meadow Pathways

Meadow Pathways Wellbeing and Education Cornwall provides trauma-informed, person-centred support for children and young people across Cornwall. We specialise in working with those experiencing Social, Emotional and Mental Health needs, Special Educational Needs, Emotionally Based School Non-Attendance, bereavement, and the effects of educational trauma.

What we offer

To support this journey, we provide a fully bespoke package of care and education that includes:

  • Mentoring and Advocacy
    Building trust and self-belief through consistent relational support and Alternative Provisions.
  • Tailored Learning Packages
    Flexible academic pathways matched to each learner’s pace and interests.
  • Life Skills and Independence Training
    Practical preparation for adulthood, including functional skills and community engagement.
  • Diagnostic Assessments
    Literacy and numeracy profiling to inform targeted intervention.
  • SEMH Interventions
    Trauma-informed strategies to support emotional regulation and resilience.
  • Wellbeing and PSHE Packages
    Creative therapeutic sessions that promote self-awareness, relationships, and personal growth.
  • Transitional Services
    Carefully planned reintegration or onward pathways, supported by multi-agency collaboration.
  • Therapeutic and Curriculum-Linked Activities
    Engaging, curriculum-linked activities tailored to interests and supported by ASDAN and AQA Unit Awards.

Our approach

We build trusting relationships through warmth, empathy, and consistency. Our practice is shaped by the SPACE ethos — Safety, Playfulness, Acceptance, Curiosity, and Empathy — and we use play therapy, creative activities, and community-based learning to help children and young people re-engage, feel safe, and grow in confidence.

Meet the team

Michelle Pascoe

Trauma-informed counselling Play therapy Emotion coaching EBSNA support Bereavement support Nurture practice

Michelle is a founding director and lead therapist for EOTAS and Meadow Pathways Wellbeing and Education Cornwall. She supports children and young people within the community who experience SEN, SEMH, educational trauma, bereavement, and EBSNA.

With over 20 years of experience, Michelle is a qualified counsellor and play therapist who has worked in specialist settings including senior leadership and as a Designated Safeguarding Lead.

Michelle works in a trauma-informed, person-centred way, focusing on building trust and emotional safety with families, schools, and professionals. Outside of work, Michelle enjoys exploring Cornwall’s coastal paths and woodlands with her dogs, Pixie and Ivey.

Zoe Waitz

Inclusive practice Outdoor learning Curriculum planning Pastoral leadership Wellbeing-focused practice

Zoe is a founding director of Meadow Pathways and brings leadership rooted in compassion and inclusive practice. She specialises in outdoor learning approaches and pastoral leadership to support vulnerable learners.

Her experience spans teaching, outdoor education and senior roles in specialist settings. Nature and community-based activities are central to Zoe’s approach to wellbeing and learning.
