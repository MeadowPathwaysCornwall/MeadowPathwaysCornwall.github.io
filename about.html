About Meadow Pathways

Guiding Individual Journeys with Care and Purpose

Reconnecting children with learning and life. Supporting individual growth through nurturing and understanding practices. We help children thrive through tailored trauma informed educational approaches. 🌿✨

Who we are

Meadow Pathways provides trauma informed, person‑centred education and wellbeing support for children and young people across Cornwall. We specialise in supporting those with SEND, SEMH, Emotionally Based School Non Attendance (EBSNA), bereavement, and the effects of educational trauma. Our ethos is rooted in safety, trust, creativity, and belonging.

We believe that every child deserves the opportunity to thrive, no matter their background or previous experiences. Our holistic approach blends therapeutic practice with flexible, creative education that meets learners where they are — emotionally, socially, and academically.

Working in partnership with families, schools, and local authorities, we design individualised programmes that nurture confidence, rebuild relationships with learning, and promote emotional resilience. Whether through outdoor exploration, creative arts, or one‑to‑one mentoring, our work empowers children and young people to rediscover their strengths and find joy in learning.

At Meadow Pathways, we recognise that healing and education go hand in hand. By fostering an environment where every voice is valued and every journey respected, we help our learners grow not only in knowledge but in wellbeing, independence, and self‑belief.

What we offer

  • 🧑‍🤝‍🧑 Mentoring and Advocacy — Consistent relational support.
  • 📚 Tailored Learning Packages — Flexible academic pathways.
  • 🛠️ Life Skills and Independence — Functional skills and community engagement.
  • 🧮 Diagnostic Assessments — Literacy and numeracy profiling.
  • 💛 SEMH Interventions — Trauma informed interventions.
  • 🎨 Wellbeing and PSHE — Creative therapeutic sessions.
  • 🔄 Transitional Services — Reintegration and onward pathways.
  • 🧠🌲🍳 Therapeutic and Curriculum Linked Activities — Nature‑based bush craft, walking, fitness, water‑based activities, fishing, climbing, cooking, play, biking, and more. Activities are linked to the curriculum and supported by ASDAN and AQA Unit Awards.

Our approach

We build trusting relationships through warmth, empathy, and consistency. Guided by the SPACE model — Safety, Playfulness, Acceptance, Curiosity, and Empathy. To support each individual child's needs we adapt our approach. These can include play therapy, creative activities, and community-based learning to help children re-engage, feel safe, and grow in confidence.

Meet the team

Michelle Pascoe 🌿

Michelle is a founding director and Lead Therapist for Meadow Pathways Wellbeing and Education Cornwall. She supports children and young people across the community who experience Special Educational Needs (SEN), Social, Emotional and Mental Health (SEMH) needs, educational trauma, bereavement, and Emotionally Based School Non‑Attendance (EBSNA).

With over 20 years of experience, Michelle is a qualified counsellor and play therapist. She spent more than a decade in an independent SEN school, beginning as a HLTA and progressing to senior leadership, where she served as Designated Safeguarding Lead for over seven years. She also dedicated 10 years to one of Cornwall’s only accredited Nurture Groups.

Michelle works in a trauma‑informed, person‑centred way, focusing on building trust and emotional safety with families, schools, and professionals. Her approach combines therapeutic insight with creativity and compassion, helping children rediscover their confidence, curiosity, and sense of belonging.

Beyond her professional role, Michelle enjoys exploring Cornwall’s coastal paths and woodlands with her two dogs, Pixie and Ivey 🐾🌊🌲.

Zoe Waitz 🌸

Zoe Waitz is a founding director of Meadow Pathways Wellbeing and Education Cornwall, an organisation built on the belief that every child deserves to feel safe, seen, and supported. Meadow Pathways offers inclusive, therapeutic education for children and young people with SEND and SEMH needs, placing wellbeing at the centre of everything it does.

Zoe began her career as a teaching assistant, qualified as a PE teacher, and moved into pastoral and senior leadership in specialist education. She brings deep insight into the realities faced by vulnerable learners and the teams who support them.

Nature is a constant thread in Zoe’s work. As a former outdoor education instructor, she sees the outdoors as a space that offers regulation, joy, and connection. Whether walking the Cornish coast, forest school, or cooking with foraged ingredients, Zoe believes nature supports young people, staff, and the wider community. 🌊🌲🍳
