Zoe Waitz

Director of Education. Zoe is a qualified teacher with over 10 years’ experience working in special education. She has supported children and young people who find mainstream settings challenging, including those with anxiety, school avoidance (EBSNA), neurodiversity, and trauma-related needs. Zoe is all about a child-centred approach—meeting each young person where they are and helping them grow in confidence, re-engage with learning, and find their own path. She’s passionate about using the outdoors and nature-based learning to build resilience, emotional wellbeing, and a genuine love of learning. Outside of work, Zoe can usually be found knee-deep in the veg patch, chatting to her chickens, or off exploring Cornwall’s wild corners with her family—ideally ending up at a good café.

Michelle Pascoe

Director of Therapies. Michelle has worked in SEN settings for over 20 years. She is a person-centred counsellor, play therapist, and senior mental health worker, trained in PACE, SPACE, and emotion coaching. Michelle has supported children and young people with SEMH needs, neurodiversity, trauma-related experiences, and behaviours that may challenge mainstream settings. Her approach is deeply compassionate and tailored, giving each individual and their family the time and support they need to navigate their unique path.

Michelle is also an experienced safeguarding lead, with a deep understanding of the complexities surrounding child protection and emotional wellbeing. She works closely with families, schools, and professionals to ensure that every child feels safe, heard, and supported. Her safeguarding work is rooted in empathy, respect, and a belief that every child deserves to thrive in a secure and nurturing environment. Outside of work, Michelle enjoys spending time with her family and walking her two dogs along the beautiful Cornish coast.
